Softball, Volleyball, & Basketball

  1. Softball
    • Played on dirt infield and a grass outfield.
    • Pitchers are required to throw underhand.
    • A softball is larger than a baseball, and the field is also smaller than a baseball field.
  2. Volleyball
    • Played on a court divided by a net, with two teams of six players each.
    • The objective is to send the ball over the net and ground it on the opponent's side of the court.
    • Players can use their hands, arms, and other parts of their body to hit the ball, but they cannot catch or throw it.
    • Each team has three hits to get the ball over the net.
  3. Basketball
    • Played on a court with a hoop at each end.
    • The objective is to score points by shooting the ball through the opponent's hoop.
    • Players can dribble the ball while moving, but they cannot run with the ball without dribbling.
